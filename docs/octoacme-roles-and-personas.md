# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile meetings, ensure process adherence, and help remove impediments for the delivery team. They coach the team on agile best practices and foster a culture of continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and facilitate sprints, standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Coach the team on agile principles and practices
- Help identify and clear blockers and impediments
- Shield the team from external distractions
- Track team metrics and support process improvements
- Facilitate effective collaboration and communication

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and efficiency
- Foster self-organization and accountability
- Enable predictable, sustainable delivery
- Promote continuous learning and adaptation

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup facilitation
- Sprint planning and retrospective meetings
- Ad-hoc coaching sessions with team members
- Weekly sync with PM and PdM on team health and impediments

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical delivery by clarifying requirements and acceptance criteria. They ensure that the team has a clear understanding of what needs to be built and why.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, analyze, and document business requirements
- Translate business needs into clear user stories and acceptance criteria
- Validate solutions against requirements and business objectives
- Ensure clarity of backlog items and reduce ambiguity
- Facilitate requirements workshops and walkthroughs
- Support testing and UAT coordination

### Goals
- Ensure accurate scope definition and shared understanding
- Minimize rework due to unclear requirements
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams
- Support data-driven decision making

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops with stakeholders
- Backlog refinement sessions with PdM and developers
- Acceptance criteria reviews
- Status updates on requirements clarification

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure user experience and design quality for product features. They advocate for user-centered design and create intuitive, accessible interfaces that meet user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Develop and maintain design systems and style guides
- Collaborate on design specifications and interaction patterns
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design
- Validate designs with users and stakeholders

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, delightful user experiences
- Ensure consistency across product interfaces
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Balance user needs with technical and business constraints

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with PdM and developers
- User research findings presentations
- Prototype walkthroughs with stakeholders
- Collaboration in sprint planning for design work

---

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Stakeholders provide input, feedback, and approvals; they represent distinct user groups or business functions (e.g., Sales, Support, Customers, Legal, Compliance). They ensure the project aligns with organizational objectives and user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Review deliverables and provide timely, constructive feedback
- Communicate business needs, constraints, and priorities
- Participate in milestone reviews and demos
- Provide domain expertise and context
- Approve key decisions and sign-offs
- Champion the project within their organizations

### Goals
- Ensure project outcomes meet business objectives
- Advocate for their users or business unit's needs
- Maintain visibility into project progress
- Support successful adoption and rollout

### Typical Communication
- Milestone review meetings and demos
- Feedback sessions on prototypes and deliverables
- Monthly or quarterly stakeholder updates
- Ad-hoc consultations for domain-specific questions

---

## Technical Lead

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide technical direction, mentor developers, and ensure quality standards in delivery. They balance architectural vision with pragmatic execution and mitigate technical risks.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical approaches and validate architectures
- Guide code reviews and ensure coding standards
- Mentor developers and provide technical guidance
- Identify and mitigate technical risks and debt
- Make technology choices and evaluate trade-offs
- Ensure security, performance, and scalability requirements are met

### Goals
- Deliver robust, maintainable technical solutions
- Build team technical capabilities
- Reduce technical debt and maintain system health
- Enable efficient, high-quality delivery

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews with developers
- Architecture discussions with PM and PdM
- Code review feedback and mentoring sessions
- Risk assessments and mitigation planning
